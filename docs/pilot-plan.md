# Pilot Plan

## Purpose

The pilot should test whether Iris improves student learning support and teacher workload in real PSC courses. The goal is not to prove every possible AI feature. The goal is to learn which tools students and teachers actually value.

## Recommended Pilot Shape

- Duration: 6-8 weeks.
- Courses: 2-3.
- Agents: 3-5.
- Students: one limited cohort or selected classes.
- Staff: motivated teachers willing to review and refine agents.
- Scope: Moodle launch, student chat, course resources, basic logs, flags, and analytics.

## Pilot Questions

- Do students use Iris more than once?
- Which questions do students ask repeatedly?
- Does Iris reduce repeated clarification questions to teachers?
- Do students use Iris for learning support or assessment outsourcing?
- Are agent responses accurate enough when grounded in PSC materials?
- What kinds of prompts cause unsafe, unhelpful, or misleading responses?
- Do teachers find the agent builder understandable?
- What analytics are genuinely useful to staff?

## Suggested Pilot Courses

Choose courses where:

- Assessment briefs generate repeated questions.
- Students need formative critique.
- Technical workflows create common blockers.
- Teachers are interested in shaping AI use.
- Course materials are already organized enough to upload.

Potential areas:

- Photography technical practice.
- Studio lighting.
- Portfolio development.
- Screen media production planning.
- Design or visual communication critique.

## Pilot Agents

Start with:

- Brief Decoder.
- Technical Tutor.
- Portfolio Critique Coach.
- Client Simulator.
- Student Support Concierge, if PSC has clean support information ready.

Avoid too many agents at the start. A small number of well-tuned agents will teach more than a large library of shallow tools.

## Timeline

### Week 0: Preparation

- Confirm pilot courses and staff.
- Confirm privacy notice and student communication.
- Confirm Moodle access path.
- Collect course resources, briefs, rubrics, and support links.
- Draft agent instructions.
- Test each agent internally.

### Week 1: Staff Setup

- Train pilot teachers.
- Review agent boundaries.
- Validate resource retrieval.
- Test likely student prompts.
- Define escalation process for flags.

### Week 2: Student Launch

- Introduce Iris in class or Moodle.
- Explain what it can and cannot do.
- Provide AI use and disclosure expectations.
- Start collecting usage and feedback.

### Weeks 3-6: Active Pilot

- Review flagged conversations weekly.
- Track common student questions.
- Tune agent instructions.
- Add or correct course resources.
- Note Moodle access friction.
- Record teacher observations.

### Week 7: Evaluation

- Survey students.
- Interview pilot teachers.
- Review usage and safety data.
- Identify top improvements.
- Recommend expand, revise, or pause.

### Week 8: Decision

- Produce pilot report.
- Choose next courses.
- Decide on technical investment.
- Confirm budget and governance needs.

## Pilot Success Criteria

The pilot is successful if:

- Students understand what Iris is for.
- Students use it for course-relevant support.
- Teachers can identify at least one concrete benefit.
- Safety issues are manageable.
- Usage costs are understood.
- Moodle launch works reliably enough.
- The team can name the next build priorities with confidence.

The pilot is not successful if:

- Students mostly use Iris to produce final assessable work.
- Teachers do not trust the output.
- Course materials are too poor or disorganized to support grounded answers.
- Privacy or safety review processes are unclear.
- Technical friction prevents normal use.

## Measurement Plan

### Quantitative

- Number of active students.
- Repeat usage rate.
- Conversations per course.
- Messages per agent.
- Most-used agents.
- Flag rate.
- Model cost per course.
- Average response time.

### Qualitative

- Student survey responses.
- Teacher notes.
- Examples of useful conversations.
- Examples of poor or unsafe responses.
- Common confusion themes.
- Moodle friction points.

## Student Survey Questions

- Did Iris help you understand your coursework or assessment?
- Which Iris tool did you use most?
- Did Iris help you improve or rethink your work?
- Were any responses confusing, wrong, or unhelpful?
- Did you understand what AI use was allowed for your assessment?
- Would you use Iris again in another course?

## Teacher Review Questions

- Did Iris reduce repeated student questions?
- Did the agent responses reflect your course expectations?
- Were the agent controls understandable?
- Which student questions surprised you?
- Did the analytics reveal anything useful?
- What would you change before expanding Iris?

## Pilot Deliverables

- Configured pilot environment.
- 3-5 working agents.
- Privacy and student use notice.
- Moodle launch path.
- Weekly review notes.
- End-of-pilot report.
- Recommendation for next stage.
